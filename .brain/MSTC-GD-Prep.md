## Section B: GD Tactical Framework (Your Fine-Tuning Layer)

You're already strong in public speaking and English. This section adds GD-SPECIFIC behaviors that differ from general speaking or debates.

### The 6 Scoring Parameters (What Evaluators Mark)

1. **Leadership signals** -- summarizing, moderating, structuring
2. **Team behavior** -- respectful collaboration, not domination
3. **Ability to structure points** -- frameworks, not random opinions
4. **Listening skills** -- building on others' points
5. **Confidence** -- assertive but not aggressive
6. **Clarity of thought** -- data > opinions

### Entry Strategy

- DO NOT jump in first. Let 1-2 people speak.
- Enter with a structural frame:
  - "Let me approach this from 3 angles -- economic, social, and technological."
  - "I'd like to structure this as short-term impact vs long-term implications."
  - "Before we go further, I think we should define what we mean by [X]."
- First impression matters -- make your FIRST intervention your BEST one.

### The 4-6 Intervention Rule

- Quality >>> Quantity. 4-6 strong, structured points beat 15 random interjections.
- Each intervention should have: POINT + EVIDENCE + LINK BACK TO TOPIC
- Example: "AI will create more jobs than it displaces. WEF projects a net gain of 78 million jobs by 2030. The real challenge is upskilling, not unemployment."

### The "Agree + Add" Method (Your Bread & Butter)

- "I agree with [name]'s point about X. To extend that, consider Y because Z."
- "That's a valid concern. Adding a dimension -- [your point]."
- "Building on what was said -- here's a real-world example that supports this..."

### The Moderator Move (When Chaos Erupts)

- Be the calm one: "Let's hear different perspectives on this."
- "I think we're going in circles. Can we identify our key agreements first?"
- "We have multiple views -- let me try to organize what we've discussed so far."

### The Inclusion Move (Evaluators Love This)

- "We haven't heard from everyone on this angle."
- "I'd like to hear [looking at a quiet person] what you think about..."
- Shows leadership WITHOUT domination.

### The Summary Close (Rare and Powerful)

- Most GDs end abruptly. If you can summarize, you WIN.
- Template: "To summarize -- our group identified 3 key points: [1], [2], [3]. While we had different views on [X], the consensus seems to be [Y]."
- Only attempt this if the GD is winding down and no one else has summarized.

### Body Language Checklist

- [ ] Sit upright, shoulders back
- [ ] Hands on table or gesturing gently (not crossed arms)
- [ ] Eye contact with ALL participants, not just the evaluator
- [ ] Nod when listening to others (shows engagement)
- [ ] Lean slightly forward when speaking (shows conviction)
- [ ] Smile when appropriate (approachability)

### ABSOLUTE DON'Ts

- DO NOT interrupt aggressively or shout over others
- DO NOT dominate -- if you've spoken 4 times and others haven't, STOP
- DO NOT take extreme positions ("I completely disagree with everything said")
- DO NOT attack a person ("You're wrong") -- attack the idea ("That perspective doesn't account for...")
- DO NOT use filler phrases ("basically", "actually", "you know")
- DO NOT look only at the evaluator -- it looks sycophantic
- DO NOT start with "I think" every time -- vary your entry phrases

### Topic 1: AI Replacing Jobs vs Creating Jobs

**One-liner:** AI automates routine tasks but creates new categories of work that didn't exist before.

**FOR (AI Creates Jobs):**
- WEF Future of Jobs 2025 projects a net gain of 78 million jobs globally by 2030 (170M created, 92M displaced)
- India's IT sector added AI/ML roles faster than it lost traditional testing jobs
- History: ATMs didn't eliminate bank tellers -- bank branches actually increased

**AGAINST (AI Displaces Jobs):**
- IMF: 40% of global jobs exposed to AI, 60% in advanced economies
- Call center and BPO sector in India already seeing headcount reduction
- Transition takes time -- workers displaced TODAY can't reskill TOMORROW

**Balanced Close:** "AI is a tool -- the outcome depends on how quickly we invest in reskilling. Countries that reskill faster win."

**Stat to Drop:** "India's AI market is projected to reach $17 billion by 2027."

**Balanced Close:** "The $5T target is achievable, but inclusive growth -- not just GDP numbers -- should be the real measure of success."

**Stat to Drop:** "India's GDP grew 7.8% in Q3 FY26, maintaining the fastest growth rate among major economies."
