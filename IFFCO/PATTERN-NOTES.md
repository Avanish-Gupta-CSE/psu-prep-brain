# Pattern Notes

## Confirmed

- The active advertisement is for `Graduate Engineer Trainee (GET) - 2026`.
- There is a `preliminary` online test in an open environment using the candidate's own laptop / computer and internet.
- Shortlisted candidates from the preliminary stage will appear for a `final` online test in a controlled environment at designated centres.
- Candidates shortlisted on the basis of the online test will be called for personal interview.
- The application form and advertisement do not expose a detailed written syllabus on the public page.

## Inferred

- For the `IT / Computer Science / AI` route, broad CS fundamentals are a safer bet than niche stack prep because the active notice is GET-based, not role-specific.
- The preliminary stage may reward speed, accuracy, and calm remote execution more than deep specialization.
- Interview fit will likely matter because GET selection is a trainability and intent filter, not only a raw test filter.

## Practical Prep Rule

- Reuse shared-core prep first.
- Do not sink time into cancelled-notice topics such as Oracle / PL-SQL / C# unless a new official source brings them back.
- Treat the application deadline and prelim-test readiness as the immediate operational risks.

## Most Likely Score / Interview Drivers

1. Broad CS / IT fundamentals executed quickly
2. Clean application and document readiness
3. Calm remote-test execution for the preliminary stage
4. Credible `why IFFCO / why GET / why PSU` framing
