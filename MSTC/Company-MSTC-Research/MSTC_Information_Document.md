# MSTC Limited – Information Document

*Prepared for MT (Systems) Interview – 2026*

 
 
 
MSTC Limited, formerly known as Metal Scrap Trade Corporation Limited, is a 
Mini-Ratna Category-I Public Sector Undertaking (PSU). While its name reflects its 
historical origins in the metal scrap industry, today the "MSTC" brand is synonymous 
with transparency in e-commerce and e-auctions. 
Historical Evolution 
• 
Establishment (1964): MSTC was incorporated on September 9, 1964, 
primarily to serve as a canalising agency for the export of ferrous scrap. 
• 
The Canalisation Era: For decades, it acted as a regulator and trader for the 
import and export of scrap and old ships for breaking. 
• 
The Digital Pivot (2000s): With the liberalisation of the Indian economy, 
MSTC’s monopoly on scrap trading ended. The company pivoted toward 
Information Technology. It developed proprietary software to conduct large-
scale electronic auctions. 
• 
The E-Commerce Giant (2020-2026): By 2026, MSTC has transitioned into a 
"Service Provider" rather than just a "Trader." It now facilitates the sale and 

 
 
procurement of a vast range of goods and services for the Government of India 
and private entities. 
Nature of Organisation 
• 
Status: Miniratna Category-I PSU. This status grants the company significant 
financial autonomy to enter into joint ventures and set up subsidiaries. 
• 
Headquarters: Kolkata, West Bengal. 
• 
Operational Presence: MSTC operates through a network of regional and 
branch offices across India, including major hubs like Delhi, Mumbai, 
Bengaluru, Chennai, and Hyderabad, ensuring a PAN-India service delivery 
model.

Role in India’s Economy 

 
 
MSTC is a key driver of Digital India. By providing a neutral, transparent, and efficient 
platform for the auctioning of natural resources (Coal, Iron Ore, and spectrum), it 
ensures that the national exchequer receives the maximum possible revenue while  
eliminating corruption and middlemen. 
How does MSTC contribute to the Indian Economy? 
• 
Revenue Generation: Helps the government get the best market price for its 
assets. 
• 
Transparency: Eliminates the "middleman" and "mafia" culture often 
associated with physical scrap auctions. 
• 
Industrial Raw Material: Ensures a steady supply of scrap for the secondary 
steel sector, reducing the need for costly iron ore mining. 
 
 
 
Ownership and Governance 

 
 
MSTC Limited is under the administrative control of the Ministry of Steel, 
Government of India. The Government maintains a majority stake, ensuring that the 
company adheres to public accountability standards while competing in a commercial 
market.

Business Operations & Products 
A. E-Commerce (The Flagship Segment) 
MSTC’s e-auction platform is the backbone of its current profitability. 
• 
E-Auction: Used for selling scrap, surplus stores, forest produce (timber), 
minerals, and agricultural products. 

 
 
• 
E-Procurement: Helping government departments buy goods and services 
The company has established itself as a pioneer in the e-auction segment, offering 
services such as: 
1. Sale of Scrap and Surplus Stores: 
Conducting e-auctions for scraps, old plant & machinery, surplus stores, and real 
estate. 
2. Power Sector Solutions: 
Facilitating power procurement, transmission contracts, and equipment purchases. 
3. Telecom Spectrum Allocation: 
Providing e-solutions for the allocation of telecom 4G/5G spectrum. 
4. Mining Sector Services: 
Providing bidding solutions for the allocation of mining leases, composite licenses, 
critical mineral allocations, exploration licenses, royalty collection, and mine 
development contracts for major mineral blocks. It also provides auction services 
for the allocation of minor mineral and sand blocks with integrated payment and 
billing solutions. 
 
 
5. EXIM Solutions: 
Developing EXIM portals for leading petroleum companies to streamline exporting 
and importing activities and better price discovery. 
6. Online Draw System (ODS): 

 
 
Developed software for an online computerised draw system for the distribution of 
LPG & Retail Petrol Pump dealership. 
7. Government Subsidy Allocations: 
Implementing e-solutions for government subsidy programs such as the Regional 
Connectivity Scheme (UDAN), RLNG, and DDUGJY/IPDS. 
8. Forestry and Agricultural Produce: 
Conducting e-auctions for forest products, agricultural produce, and other natural 
resources. 
9. Duty Scrips Management: 
Managing duty scrips for various sectors. 
10. Coal Sector Services: 
Facilitating coal auctions, coal block allocation, the Shakti Scheme, and coal 
gasification projects. 
11. e-Procurement Services: 
MSTC has also developed a tendering portal for the procurement of Goods, Services 
& Works, which is being used by various Government & Private clients. 
B. Trading Operations 
Though decreasing as a percentage of total revenue to minimise risk, MSTC still 
facilitates trading in: 
• 
Bulk Raw Materials: Coal, Iron Ore, and Coke. 
• 
Industrial Raw Materials: Sourcing requirements for small and medium 
enterprises (SMEs). 

 
 
• 
Agricultural Products: Occasionally facilitating the import/export of essential 
commodities like oilseeds or pulses as per government directives. 
C. New-Age Digital Services 
•

(e.g., the "Property Auction" portal for banks to sell NPA assets). 
• 
Logistics Tracking: Integration of GST and e-way bill systems into their auction 
platforms for seamless movement of goods.

Market Position 
MSTC holds a leadership position in the G2B (Government-to-Business) e-auction 
space. Because it is a government entity, it enjoys a "Trust Advantage." Government 
departments prefer MSTC because its processes are pre-vetted by the CVC (Central 
Vigilance Commission). 
Key Competitors 

 
 
1. Public Sector: GeM (Government e-Marketplace). While GeM is for standard 
procurement (pens, laptops, cars), MSTC specialises in complex auctions 
(minerals, scrap, NPA assets). 
2. Private Sector: mjunction (a 50:50 JV between Tata Steel and SAIL). 
mjunction is the primary competitor in the steel and coal e-auction space. 
3. B2B Platforms: Private auctioneers like Shriram Automall (for vehicles) and 
various fintech platforms for bank auctions. 
Industry Trends (2025–2026) 
• 
Transparency over Speed: The shift from physical "sealed bids" to 
"Forward/Reverse E-Auctions." 
• 
The Circular Economy: With the 2026 emphasis on "Net Zero," MSTC’s role 
in recycling (scrap) is no longer just business; it is environmental stewardship. 
• 
AI and Data Analytics: Using data from 20 years of auctions to predict price 
trends for minerals and scrap.

Ministry & Regulatory Framework 
The Role of the Ministry of Steel 
While MSTC is a digital service provider, it remains under the Ministry of Steel because 
of its historical link to the steel scrap cycle. The Ministry provides strategic direction, 
especially regarding the National Steel Policy and the Scrappage Policy. 
Regulatory Compliance 
1. CVC Guidelines: All MSTC portals must be compliant with the Central 
Vigilance Commission’s guidelines for transparency. 

 
 
2. STQC Certification: Their software is regularly audited by the Standardisation 
Testing and Quality Certification (STQC) Directorate to ensure it is hack-proof 
and fair. 
3. DPE Guidelines: As a PSU, it follows the Department of Public Enterprises 
(DPE) norms regarding HR, salaries, and corporate governance. 
 
Recent Projects & Expansion Plans 
Centralised Travel Booking Platform (2026 Launch) 
MSTC is developing a comprehensive, centralised travel portal for government 
employees. This platform aims to standardise air, rail, and hotel bookings across various 
ministries, ensuring cost-efficiency and direct billing, similar to corporate travel portals 
but on a massive government scale. 
Expansion in EPR (Extended Producer Responsibility) Services 
Under the Ministry of Environment’s guidelines, MSTC has expanded its portal to 
manage EPR certificates. They provide a marketplace where producers (of plastic, e-
waste, battery waste, and tyres) can buy EPR certificates from recyclers to meet their 
legal environmental obligations. 
Gold Bullion Allocation Platform 
In collaboration with the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT), MSTC has 
launched/refined a digital platform for the allocation of gold bullion. This brings 
transparency to how precious metals are imported and distributed to domestic jewellers 
and exporters. 
 
 

 
 
 
RERA & Property Auctions 
MSTC has become the preferred partner for various State RERA (Real Estate 
Regulatory Authority) bodies to auction off stalled projects or properties of defaulting 
developers to compensate homebuyers. 
 
CSR Activities & Sustainability Focus 
• 
Sustainability & Recycling: MSTC is the leader in the organised scrap 
recycling sector. By promoting the recycling of steel and vehicles, they 
significantly reduce the carbon footprint of the metal industry. 
• 
Digital Transformation as Sustainability: By moving all government 
disposals to a "Paperless E-Auction" format, MSTC has saved millions of tons 
of paper and reduced the need for physical travel for bidders. 
• 
Community Development: 
o Education: Funding smart classrooms in rural Bengal and other 
operational areas. 
o Healthcare: Support for mobile medical units and sanitation projects 
under the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan. 
• 
ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance): MSTC is increasingly being 
rated on ESG parameters, focusing on ethical governance and "Green 
Procurement" processes. 

 
 
 
Important News & Developments (2025–2026) 
• 
Mining Auctions: MSTC has successfully conducted auctions for "Critical and 
Strategic Minerals" (like Lithium and Rare Earth Elements), which are vital for 
India’s EV battery mission. 
• 
Partnership with Banks: Expanded tie-ups with major PSBs (Public Sector 
Banks) for the e-auction of mega-stressed assets, helping reduce NPAs (Non-
Performing Assets) in the banking sector. 
Current Challenges & Future Opportunities 
Challenges 
1. Competition: Increasing competition from private e-commerce players and 
specialised auction houses like mjunction. 

 
 
2. Cybersecurity: As a platform handling transactions worth billions, the threat of 
cyberattacks and data breaches is a constant challenge. 
3. Policy Dependence: Any change in government procurement policies or 
export/import duties on scrap can directly impact their bottom line. 
Opportunities 
1. International Expansion: There is a massive opportunity to export MSTC’s e-
auction software model to developing nations in Africa and Southeast Asia. 
2. Carbon Credits: Developing a platform for trading Carbon Credits as India 
moves towards Net Zero 2070. 
3. AI & Data Analytics: Using AI to predict scrap price trends and buyer 
behaviour to provide "Business Intelligence" as a value-added service.
