# MSTC – Domain Based Questions (MT Systems Focus)

*Compiled from PYQs and expected technical questions*

 
 
FINANCE & ACCOUNTS DOMAIN 

### Q1. How does MSTC recognize revenue from e-auction services? 
MSTC earns revenue from e-auctions primarily through service charges and auction fees. This 
revenue is recognized when the auction is completed and the service has been delivered. In simple 
terms, once MSTC completes the auction process and the fee becomes payable, revenue is recorded. 
Generally, EMD of unsuccessful bidders is refunded within 7 to 10 working days after auction closure, 
depending on the reconciliation and approval process. 

### Q3. Explain the auction success fee calculation structure. 
Auction success fee is usually calculated as a fixed percentage of the final sale value. For example, if 
MSTC charges 1% and the auction value is ₹10 crore, then MSTC earns ₹10 lakh as service fee, plus 
applicable taxes. 

### Q4. How does MSTC account for GST on e-procurement services? 
MSTC charges GST on the service fee collected from clients. This GST is recorded separately as tax 
liability and later deposited with the government after tax adjustments. 

### Q5. What is MSTC’s dividend payout ratio trend? 
MSTC generally follows a stable dividend payout trend as it is a PSU. It aims to balance shareholder 
return with business growth and government expectations. 

### Q6. How do you calculate ROI for a scrap auction event? 
ROI means Return on Investment. It is calculated by comparing profit earned from auction against 
total cost incurred. 
Formula is: Profit divided by Cost into 100. 
This shows whether the auction was financially efficient. 

### Q7. Difference between trading revenue and service fee revenue? 
Trading revenue comes when MSTC buys and sells goods directly. 
Service fee revenue comes when MSTC only facilitates auction or procurement and earns 
commission without owning goods. 
 

### Q8. Auction generates ₹50 crore, but bidder defaults on payment—what accounting treatment? 
In this case, full revenue should not be recognized immediately because payment is not realized. 
EMD may be forfeited, expected revenue may be reversed or deferred, and the material may go for 
re-auction. 

### Q9. Client disputes service charges—how do you reconcile? 
I would first check the contract terms, auction records, fee calculation, and tax details. Then I would 
discuss with the client using proper supporting documents to resolve the issue transparently. 

 
 

### Q10. Forex fluctuation impacts imported scrap deal—hedging strategy? 
To manage forex risk, we can use forward contracts, fix exchange rates in advance, and monitor 
currency movement regularly to reduce losses. 
 
PERSONNEL & ADMINISTRATION DOMAIN 

### Q11. How would you design an MT training program for auction operations? 
I would design it in three parts: first, company induction and MSTC business understanding; second, 
department rotation for practical exposure; third, live auction learning with reporting and 
assessment. 

### Q12. What KPI framework for e-auction executives? 
KPIs can include the number of successful auctions, revenue generated, bidder participation, dispute 
resolution time, and client satisfaction. 

### Q13. How to handle inter-departmental conflicts? 
I would handle it through clear communication, role clarity, regular coordination meetings, and focus 
on common organizational goals. 

### Q14. Succession planning strategy for retiring auction managers? 
I would identify junior talent early, give them mentoring, document key processes, and ensure 
smooth knowledge transfer before retirement. 

### Q15. Design employee engagement for PAN-India MSTC workforce. 
Employee engagement can be improved through digital town halls, recognition programs, learning 
sessions, wellness activities, and regular feedback channels. 
 

### Q16. Office administration challenges for Kolkata HQ + branches? 
The main challenges are coordination, uniform processes, infrastructure management, vendor 
handling, and smooth communication across all offices. 

### Q17. How to streamline travel approvals for auction teams? 
Travel approvals can be improved through a digital approval system, a predefined travel policy, a 
faster approval workflow, and centralized expense tracking. 

### Q18. Records management policy for auction documents? 
A strong records system should include digitization, proper indexing, secure storage, retention policy, 
and easy retrieval for audit or legal needs. 
 
SYSTEMS (IT) DOMAIN 

 
 

### Q19. What security architecture does MSTC use? 
secure login, and compliance with CVC and STQC standards. 

### Q20. Difference between an e-auction portal and an e-tender portal? 
The e-auction portal is used for competitive price bidding and price discovery. 
The e-tender portal is used for procurement, where both technical and financial bids are evaluated. 

### Q21. Aadhaar/DigiLocker integration for bidder KYC? 
Aadhaar and DigiLocker can be used through API integration for identity verification, document 
authentication, OTP-based validation, and secure KYC storage. 

### Q22. Load balancing for 10,000 concurrent bidders? 
This can be managed using load balancers, distributed servers, session control, database 
optimization, and backup systems for uninterrupted performance. 

### Q23. API integration for GST/e-way bill? 
API integration helps validate GST details, generate invoices, calculate tax, and connect with the e-
way bill system for smooth compliance. 
 

### Q24. DDoS attack during live coal auction—response? 
Immediate response should be traffic filtering, DDoS protection activation, failover systems, cyber 
team alert, and maintaining business continuity. 

### Q25. Fake bid placement complaint—investigation? 
I would check login history, IP address, timestamps, session logs, and authentication trail to verify 
whether the bid was genuine or unauthorized. 

### Q26. Cloud migration checklist? 
Checklist includes data backup, application mapping, security review, compliance check, testing, 
disaster recovery setup, and phased migration. 
 
LAW DOMAIN 

### Q27. Key CVC guidelines for MSTC e-auctions? 
Main CVC principles are transparency, fairness, equal opportunity, audit trail, and a non-
discriminatory bidding process. 

### Q28. IT Act 2000 compliance for auction platforms? 
Compliance includes valid electronic records, digital signatures, secure systems, legal recognition of 
e-contracts, and cyber protection. 

 
 

### Q29. Contract enforcement for a defaulting bidder? 
The process includes notice issuance, EMD forfeiture, penalty as per terms, possible blacklisting, and 
legal action if required. 

### Q30. Role of DPE guidelines in HR policies? 
DPE guidelines guide PSU HR policies related to promotion, conduct, pay structure, vigilance, and 
employee governance. 
 

### Q31. STQC audit frequency and scope? 
STQC audits are periodic and usually cover security, compliance, system performance, and process 
validation. 

### Q32. Data localization under the DPDP Act? 
Bidder personal data should be securely stored, lawfully processed, access-controlled, and managed 
according to Indian data protection requirements. 

### Q33. Blacklisting procedure? 
Blacklisting should follow a fair process—notice, hearing opportunity, evidence review, final order, 
and appeal provision. 
 
OPERATIONS DOMAIN 

### Q34. End-to-end e-auction lifecycle? 
The process is: client onboarding, listing, approval, bidder registration, EMD submission, auction 
publication, live bidding, closure, payment, and reporting. 

### Q35. Bid evaluation criteria? 
First, technical eligibility is checked, then financial bids of qualified bidders are evaluated for the best 
commercial outcome. 

### Q36. Dispute resolution mechanism? 
Disputes are handled through complaint review, document verification, system logs, committee 
review, and final resolution. 

### Q37. How to increase auction participation? 
Participation can be increased through better bidder outreach, easy onboarding, training webinars, 
digital promotion, and mobile-friendly access. 

### Q38. Last-minute bidder withdrawal? 
I would assess competition impact, apply EMD rules, and decide whether to continue or rework the 
strategy based on auction competitiveness. 

 
 
 
 

 
 

### Q39. Multiple highest bidders due to a glitch? 
Auction should be paused, logs reviewed, bidders informed, and the issue resolved transparently 
through an audit-backed decision or re-bid. 

### Q40. Re-auction decision criteria? 
Re-auction depends on low participation, poor price discovery, technical issues, market conditions, 
and client approval.
