## Expectation calibration (as of 15 May)

Field intel suggests some recent Systems interviews can be **very short** and look like: **intro → basic DBMS/CN/OS/DSA → done**, with minimal/no HR. Public MSTC interview experiences also mention short durations like “**6–8 minutes**” in some cases. [MSTC Interview Questions and Answers (AmbitionBox)](https://www.ambitionbox.com/interviews/mstc-interview-questions)

Candidate updates also suggest:
- panel can be large (example: **8** officers), but questions may still be rapid-fire
- duration can vary by time-of-day (example: **~7 minutes** late slots; others report **~10–13 minutes**)
- beyond pure CS, you may get 1–2 quick company-fit prompts like **type of auction** and **competitors**

So we prepare for a **7‑minute lightning round** and keep a **10–15 minute extension pack** ready.

## Non‑negotiables (every day until interview)

- **Lightning mock (2×/day)**: run the **7-minute** simulation from `MSTC/TECHNICAL-QUESTION-BANK.md` (section “7-minute lightning mode”).
- **Core technical (3 blocks/day)**: DBMS + OS + CN + DSA fundamentals (active recall + recordings).
- **1 mini‑mock/day** (timed): 10–15 questions mixed.
- **1 repair loop/day**: rewrite + re‑record the weakest 5 answers.
- **Mirror practice (10 min/day)**: intro only (and 1 case only if time).
- **Company/governance (15–20 min/day)**: keep it “interview-useful only” (MSTC in 1 line + forward vs reverse auction + 1–2 competitors). Pull everything else only if time remains.

## Study method (fastest way to become “interview-ready”)

For every topic block:
1) **10 min compressed theory** (keywords only)  
2) **25–35 min Q-drill** (speak answers, record)  
3) **10 min repair** (rewrite the 3 weakest answers, retake)

Output per block (must exist on paper/notes):
- **1-page cheat sheet** (keywords)
- **Top-5 weak list** (to revisit next day)
