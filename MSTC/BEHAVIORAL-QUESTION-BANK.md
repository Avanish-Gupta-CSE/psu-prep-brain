# Behavioral Question Bank

Use a STAR structure, but answer in clean PSU interview language rather than startup jargon.

## Likely Behavioral Questions

### Ownership
- Tell us about a time you took ownership of an important task.
- Best story candidates: ReservesAI async pipeline, STMicro eRFP-NPI buildout

### Learning Fast
- Tell us about a time you had to learn a new technology quickly.
- Best story candidates: Azure Document Intelligence, Elasticsearch, Keycloak / OpenFGA exposure

### Pressure Handling
- Tell us about a time you delivered under pressure.
- Best story candidates: production-facing Berkadia work, STMicro delivery pipeline work

### Problem Solving
- Tell us about a difficult technical problem you solved.
- Best story candidates: eRFP-NPI scaling, DMS authorization and document workflow design

### Conflict / Disagreement
- Tell us about a time you disagreed with a teammate or stakeholder.
- Best story candidates: architecture or workflow decision discussions in Berkadia projects

### Mistake / Failure
- Tell us about a mistake and what you learned from it.
- Candidate angle: over-preparation, delayed action, or a technical debugging miss that improved process discipline

### Quality Focus
- Tell us about a time you improved quality or reliability.
- Best story candidates: 85%+ test coverage in CMS, 100% pass rate after security-fix work at STMicro

### Handling Ambiguity
- Tell us about a time requirements were unclear.
- Best story candidates: new product modules or evolving enterprise workflows

### Team Contribution
- Tell us about a time you helped the team succeed beyond your own assigned work.
- Best story candidates: test harness support, CI/CD setup, security cleanup

### Initiative
- Tell us about a time you did more than what was asked.
- Best story candidates: building reusable tools or improving processes instead of only completing assigned tickets

## Answer Rules

- Lead with the situation in one or two lines only.
- Spend most time on your action, not generic team activity.
- End with a measurable result if one exists in the resume.
- If no hard metric exists, use a believable operational result.
